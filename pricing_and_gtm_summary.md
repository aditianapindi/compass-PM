# Compass: Pricing & Go-to-Market — How We Got Here

## Business Model: Freemium Subscription (not cohort)

We studied every competitor's model. Cohort programs (PM School at INR 39,999, Growth School at INR 10-30K) generate strong one-time revenue but create a "graduation cliff" — zero retention after the program ends. GrowthX validated the subscription pivot, moving from a INR 75K cohort to INR 15-20K/year and growing to 5,600+ members. Exponent proved subscription works in NA at $144/year. The PM career switch takes 6-18 months — a subscription captures that full journey and aligns our economics with user success.

## Pricing: INR 999/mo or INR 7,999/year (save 33%)

India's WTP ceiling is ~INR 40,000, proven by NextLeap's pricing. The sweet spot for subscription is INR 8,000-15,000/year — below INR 3,000, users mentally classify you as "free content" and engagement drops. Our INR 7,999/year sits at the sweet spot floor, making it an easy yes for engineers earning INR 8-15 LPA. The monthly option at INR 999 serves the "try before committing" segment while still pricing above the free-content threshold.

## Freemium Split: Free diagnosis, paid navigation

Users get Landing → Onboarding → Fit Verdict → Resume Upload → Gate Task → Readiness Score for free. This takes ~5 minutes and delivers a concrete, personalized score — our TTFV moment. The paywall sits between diagnosis (knowing your gaps) and navigation (fixing them). This mirrors the highest-conversion trigger we found in research: the moment a user sees a specific, quantified gap, urgency to act peaks.

## Beachhead: India first, NA in Year 2

India wins on four factors: (1) lower CAC — reachable through LinkedIn, The Product Folks (119K+), and WhatsApp prep groups at under $10 CAC vs. $50+ in NA; (2) wider white space — PM School's acquisition by NextLeap left a gap, and no persistent platform exists; (3) coaching culture fit — paying for structured guidance is normalized (JEE coaching analogy); (4) lower burn — faster iteration at lower cost. The counter-argument is NA's 3-5x higher LTV ($300-600 vs $120-200), but for a first-time founder without NA distribution, India is the safer validation path. Architecture is built for both markets from day one to avoid India-only lock-in.

## Target Segment

Indian career switchers (engineers/analysts), 2-5 years experience, targeting Flipkart/Meesho/Razorpay/Swiggy/CRED. Active pool: 5,000-10,000 at any given time, within a broader market of 50,000-80,000 aspirants/year competing for ~2,000-3,500 junior PM openings (15-25x oversubscription).
